Brighton Pavilion Estate

The Royal Pavilion Estate is an iconic feature of Brighton and draws in tourists throughout the year. The estate consists of:

  • Royal Pavilion
  • Brighton Museum & Art Gallery
  • Pavilion Garden
  • Brighton Dome Concert Hall
  • Corn Exchange and Studio Theatre

History

George IV first visited Brighton in 1783 and as a result commissioned the construction of Carlton House in 1787. This contained three main rooms: a breakfast room, dining room and library. Carlton House was extended many times from 1801 to 1808 becoming the Marine Pavilion. Between 1815 and 1822 the designer John Nash redecorated and extended the Pavilion, and remains in the state that he designed it in today

Uses

During WW1 was transformed into a military hospital. It treated soldiers from the Indian Army and consisted of 2 operating theatres and over 720 beds.

Today it remains a tourist attraction with tours of the Pavilion being given. The Art Gallery offers a historical view of Brighton.
